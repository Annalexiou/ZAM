1.Meditation


According to the Humanity University, meditation trains our mind and helps us focus and redirect our thoughts.
It helps increase the awareness of our surroundings and ourselves, as we tend to be constandly carried away in our own busy lives.
Meditation can give you a sense of calm, peace and balance that can benefit your emotional well-being and your overall health.

Meditating is easy!

Here are some simple steps to help you start...

First, you must find a quiet and comfortable space.
This could be a place where you can relax without being interrupted or distracted.
Then you should sit comfortably.
You can use a chair, a cushion, or even your bed. It is important to maintain good posture without feeling strained.
At last, close your eyes and take deep breaths whilst paying attention to the movement of your breath and let your thoughts flow freely.
After doing this for a few minutes, you will start feeling a sesne of calm and relaxation.

2. Exercise


Working out regularly is one of the best ways to relax your body and mind.
During exercise and other physical activities, your body produces endorphins.
These are natural chemicals in the brain that help relieve pain, reduce stress, help you sleep and improve your sense of wellbeing.


Here are some exersices that you could do:

  • Go out for a walk or run
  • Take a bike ride
  • Do a workout at home
  • Do some physical housework or gardening
  • Visit a free outdoor gym
  • Sign up to a sports team.

3.Social support

Having supportive people in your life is the key to stress management.
If you lack emotional support and friendship, it's important to get it. That may mean reaching out to your existing network.
Perhaps confiding in a family member or distant friend can help you become closer and it may give you the social support you need.
You may also need to expand your network.
Join an organization, attend a support group, or get professional help if you lack supportive people in your life.
